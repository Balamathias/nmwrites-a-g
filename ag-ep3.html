The Abandoned Genius

Episode 3

Now I could no longer experience those warm nights and cold mornings with mom!

Mom was gone for good!

At some point I got famished. There was nothing to eat!

At five I developed a feeling of the need to know but my stomach could not spare me to think.

I gently marched around the forest, examining it's trees.

After walking a distance from where I had spent the previous night, I could not secure anything that was good for the stomach.

I walked even farther and farther. After walking miles, I clashed with a small stream that streamed down the forest.

I quenched my thirst from the crystal clear water.

After I had stood up from my knees, I sensed as if someone was behind me, fear gripped me. I turned gently to behold which ever monster was staring.

To my surprise, I saw a bird feeding from a guava fruit! Although I couldn't conclude whether it was food or not but I sensed that if birds would eat it to survive, I will definitely do too.

I could not spare a step as I ran to the guava and ate to my satisfaction.

_______

At evening I'd be cuddled by the east wind, at night I would weep. At day I would yearn for survival.

This became my routine every single day in the woods.

At this point, my bones couldn't stop stretching as the sun set and rose on me.

Those season of fear had to leave! The trauma of fear was coerced out of my being as days turned months and years.

Every morning, I'd wake and listen to the melodious voices of those birds that kept me company at day. I was always sad not having them at night.

I also played around with butterflies. I gave names to birds and insects. Whenever I saw the same insect again, I'd recall it's name.

Overtime, guava exited it's season, I had to stick to another food supply. I ate foods that were never tagged food.

At seven, I had already adapted to my new environment. My specialty was none the less hidden.

I knew at this point the world I was! Somehow, The memory of mom was gone.

I was facing reality, though with dreams.

I was a wasting genius!!!
