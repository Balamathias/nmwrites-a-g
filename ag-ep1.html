The Abandoned Genius

Episode One

It was the middle of summer that I first opened my eyes to behold the new world I had appeared in and the applauding hands that rejoiced over my coming.

I could recall the surrounding mysteries of my birth that led to my tender exposure to the cruel fate of nature.

Many said I was born with a tooth, unlike other children in my little and tender community.

I emerged from a system that held some supernatural beliefs about the birth of Children.

Children whose birth were as mysterious as mine were either executed or abandoned in the forest that the little village had lost renowned hunters to.

To add to the pack, I also had natural blue eyes! This aroused suspicion among the community leaders who held the customs and traditions of my culture.

Rather than lament over what had ensued, my parents were rather happy as they saw me to be a unique child.

After about eleven days of my birth, I could speak! I could pronounce words rather than cry.

This event turned the whole world against me! It had never happened before in the entire community and the spread out regions!

"That boy must be cursed!" One of the elders exclaimed.

"He will surely bring badluck to the community if he is not given to the "evil forest". Another suggested.

The rest of the leaders took counsel and drew a conclusion that I should be abandoned in the evil forest.

I was only eleven days old!

When it was the following day, my Parents were summoned by the leaders of the community. The resolution of the council was laid affront them.

In my culture, one dare not reject the resolution of the council as it was believed to have emerged from experienced traditionalists.

"The child must not spend more than two weeks in the land!" One of the elders stressed.

"Yes!" The rest chorused.

The community Chief was pleased with the council's resolution. He presided over the matter and rendered his judgement in their favour.

My parents were dumbfounded, unable to utter their exercisable freedom.

Since it was already the twelfth day, I was due to be sent to the woods.

I was seized from my own Parents by some mighty men of valor on the order of the council, cuddled and saddled like a horse up to the woods.

I was left all alone, wrapped in the very first linen that touched my body.

In my innocence, I smiled to the butterflies that hovered over my head.

I could enjoy the melodious sweetness of the tunes of the singing birds. The breeze that led the dry leaves brushed over me and made a companion the dry leaves to my tiny home.

I was left to the view of the forest trees, a baby to crave for life at such a tender age!
