The Abandoned Genius

Episode 4

Overtime, I grew from strength to strength and from wisdom to wisdom. I always felt a greater force before and behind me that helped me survive the dreadful, monstrous and deadly forest.

At nine, I had a near death experience. I've always been quiet all the time, my presence had never been noticed before but this time, I was rendered vulnerable.

______

It was a season of technology and science advancement when an helicopter flew above the forest. At it's distant sound I thought a monster or maybe the most dreadful of all monsters was on it's way.

I ran to my hay and hid there.

I watched from a distance how the helicopter spinned across. I was very much impressed with the sight. I had a feeling that somehow, humans were mostly responsible for it. I gently let go of the first helicopter as I sat in my hay thinking about it.

Just then, I saw a dragon fly dangling in the air. I stared it closely and then compared it to what I had seen earlier. Many thoughts led to the other and I went as far as comparing it with birds.

After sixteen days of meditating on what I had seen, I was able to deduce in my own primary experiment the reason why that thing could swim in air without falling.

______

After a few more days, I yearned to see the helicopter again. Luckily for me, I was able to. This time, when I heard the sound from a distance, I ran out of my hay towards the direction of the helicopter.

I shouted and screamed for help but nay, it went by. I lost it! Still with my eyes hung above the sky, facing the heavens and staring the direction it went. I brought my head back down but to my surprise, I saw myself staring a tiger!

______

I couldn't wait for it to start chasing, I ran for my dear life. The tiger took after me! I struggled through the forest and it chased too. I lounged through the forest trees and shrubs but it was just few meters behind me.

I was almost giving up! I had concluded that my life was over at this point. To worsen the case, I slipped and fell! I had prepared myself for death. I found it hard to lift up myself again.

______

Fortunately and miraculously, the Tiger jumped over me. When I stretched forth my eyes to see what it was chasing, I realized that it was an antelope. I heaved a sigh of safety that sprawled the dry leaves on the ground. I dusted my worn out attires and headed for my hay.

I couldn't trace my hay anymore! I remained amidst the forest wandering around and walking helter skelter. Yet with dreams, I found a solace there, at least I could lay my head. I hoped to dream dreams of another world as the sun went dark on me.

At this point, I had lost the memory of my background. I only yearned for a better life.

I was a wasting genius!
